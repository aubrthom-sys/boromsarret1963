13:26

The wagoner and the suited passenger ride toward the camera before a police whistle abruptly interrupts them. A close-up shows a police officer blowing the whistle. The cart stops as the horse neighs. The officer approaches, demanding “Let’s see your papers.” The suited passenger quietly exits the cart and leaves the scene. The officer continues, “Have you a permit to come up here? No carts are allowed here!” The wagoner hands over his papers while the officer begins writing a citation. As the papers are unfolded, a medal falls to the ground. The wagoner bends to retrieve it, but the officer deliberately places his foot over it. Looking down, the officer commands, “Beat it! Fast!” The wagoner looks up in confusion, his clenched hand still hovering over the hidden medal. While the officer writes the ticket, the suited passenger enters a nearby automobile without paying. The sequence ends with a low-angle composition of the wagoner beneath the officer, paralleled by a shot from below of a tall building that pans upward.

17:09

A wide shot shows the wagoner’s backyard. His wife, carrying their baby on her back, bends over peeling something unidentified into a bowl. Through the open gate, the wagoner enters leading the horse. The camera pans as he returns the horse to its place. The film cuts to the wife glaring at him as he admits, “I don’t have anything for you. Not even a cart!” She stands and silently carries the bowl inside. Two young boys enter the yard. The wife returns, hands the baby to the wagoner, and tells him “I promise you we’ll eat tonight.” She leaves the yard with the boys following ahead of her, telling them, “Go away! There’s nothing here!” The wagoner holds the crying baby as his wife exits out of the gate and the film closes.
